Helpful or Harmful? — From a Student's Perspective

Soha Noorani August 25th, 2025

As technology is rapidly expanding and advancing, there are many outlets students visit to find assistance in navigating their day-to-day lives. One of the most popular ones is AI, artificial intelligence.

I'm sure many of you have heard of different AI's such as Quill Bot, Otter AI, and most likely, Chat GPT. Different AI apps are applicable for different purposes, but all allow for quick and effective responses by having the power to scan through the entire web just to curate the perfect answer directed towards your prompt.

Now, many of us already know that, as much as AI is helpful, it is also harmful. It often influences students to eschew the development of academic skills when it comes to assignments and projects, and is also harmful to the environment. On average, Google Data Center uses about 450,000 gallons of water a day due to the use of artificial intelligence, and could reach billions of cubic meters by 2027. This does not take into account energy consumption, carbon footprint, e-waste, and much more.

Now the real question all comes down to is how us students can use AI in the most effective way to make sure we are…

  1. activating our brain and skills hand in hand with AI,
  2. using AI in the most responsible way, and
  3. finding ways to use AI to prevent environmental destruction. (Would you believe me if I didn't ask ChatGPT to write this?).

If you are an avid AI user but feel like you're not using it in a way that is fair, one of the best things to do is personalize your AI. You might be thinking, "Personalize it? It's not a coffee order or a phone case?" But it is a tool that can understand its user and analyze their skills, preferences, and how they think. You can literally feed your AI all the background and context it needs for you, so the next time you use it, instead of spitting out an abundance of information, it gives you relevant help tailored to you.

Another tip, which may sound surprising at first, is to set boundaries with your AI. What this means is to initially tell your AI tool that you do not want it to give you overloads of information to your questions, and to not answer questions that you can easily find by a simple Google search, or even through common knowledge. This allows for smart habits to be formed and less artificial intelligence to be used, while gaining more use from it each time.

Lastly, to prevent all the harm that stems from AI, simply use it less. It sounds obvious, I know, but we often overlook the most efficient option to favor the convenient. Taking into account the previous tips can help AI usage go down, but it can still be reduced more than we think. Instead, use smaller AI models, or research topics that you know you will need to know of in the near future. Find ways to enhance this skill of research and collecting data before you resort to artificial intelligence. Only a few years ago, AI was not a common part of our lives, and yet it was still a manageable era. It was possible before, and it is possible now. While artificial intelligence is expanding, so is research through articles, books, and other websites. Everything you need is all there, right at your fingertips. Use this to your advantage, and don't rely on Chat GPT for every little thing.

Your brain is wildly capable and an extraordinary tool — never forget that!

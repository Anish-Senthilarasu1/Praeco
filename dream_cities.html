Do "Dream Cities" Still Exist: How to Find Yours

Yaseen Aswad August 9th, 2025

A recent update from the website, worldsbestcities.com ranked the top 100 American cities, using Resonance Consultancy's unique analysis methods. However, after looking through their list, I soon realized that the top three cities according to their ranking just so happen to be the three largest cities in the United States by population: New York City, Los Angeles, and Chicago.

That really begs the question of what actually makes the "best" city. Is it just based on the pure number of citizens living in the area, or is there something more to uncover? Evidently, as the population of a region grows, access to higher-quality living increases as well; new and unique experiences become more readily available, from the food to the overall culture. At the same time, an increase in population also leads to overcrowding, increased pricing, and homelessness. So, how do these cities stay on top?

They don't, or at least they are slowly descending in their stature.

The "American Dream" that many of these cities had been essential in constructing appears to be more of an illusion than it seems. Especially for many Gen Z youth striving for success, that has so long been attributed to that luxury New York City apartment or the Los Angeles beachside condo has now started to shift; this has made many top city lists that just highlight the "big and flashy" have become obsolete.

Many like myself hope for something more sincere; however, we still value that aspect of adventure and thrill that a highly populated city brings. So, how can we find our best dream city? There is no "best city" in a factual sense; however, by creating simple personal criteria for a "dream city," we can then narrow down the options.

I've narrowed down three main criteria that will help anyone determine what their "dream city" is:

Environment

One of the most important factors when considering what someone's "dream city" is, is the physical environment of that area. This generally means the weather, the natural scene, and the overall physical surroundings. Some like the intense 110-degree temperatures of Austin, while others prefer the cool and breezy 70-degree temperatures of San Diego; those physical differences in temperature can make a drastic difference for most. Similarly, whenever you step outside your house and view mountainous regions, coastal beaches, or city skylines, it makes a difference in how you feel and your overall quality of life.

Culture

Just as important as the physical environment of any city is the cultural environment, the overall "vibe" or "atmosphere" that creates the appeal of a small cottage town or the aspect that draws you to a vibrant and bustling city. The food and culture surrounding it can have such a big impact on the overall appeal of a place. It is what makes the Cajun food scene of New Orleans so appealing, or how a simple New York pizza slice can mean so much. This aspect is usually most present in those "big and flashy" cities that make the top of many lists due to their sheer population and diverse cultural communities that allow for everyone to find something that they enjoy. While some value the thrill of living in a bustling city with the top restaurants and attractions that bring them the most thrill, others prefer the charm of a small town that provides the exact atmosphere they are looking for.

Potential

As much as anyone wants to live somewhere just for pure enjoyment and fun, we must consider the potential of a certain city. More specifically, it's economic opportunities for work and our future careers as a whole. Whether it would allow us to "make it big" within our lives is just as important as the other two aspects. As dynamics shift, we've seen an increase in population sizes in Texas and Florida as the number of opportunities in certain fields dwindle in California and New York. Cities such as Georgetown, Texas, and Cape Coral, Florida, have seen booms in population growth over the past few years as they become further suburbanized, and with that, more opportunities arise.

You might be wondering why this even matters for most teens; is having a Dream City really important? It might not be necessary for survival, but having a Dream City gives you something to aspire to and work hard for. If you want to make it out of where you grew up, to live somewhere that fits exactly who you are, you need a Dream City. More specifically, one that caters to the things that would help you make the most of your life. You have all the criteria you need; now it's up to you to find your Dream City.

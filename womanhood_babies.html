Fears of Womanhood: Babies

Alyssa Stephens June 19th, 2025

Fear usually dictates my life in mysterious and debilitating ways. Whether it be homework, work-work, or anything, there is always a nagging fear, about literally anything — even things that do not affect me as of now. These fears usually rotate from failure, to loss, to heights, but my current fixation has been babies.

Babies have been a recent addition to my life, as I work with small children daily, as well as babysit on the side. But a visit from my baby cousin had solidified my fears of motherhood indefinitely. Seeing my cousin and his wife handle their baby with such love, care, and attentiveness melted my heart. Watching her run around exploring the house, pointing and laughing at the fish, and appreciating my mom's collection of roosters softened my eyes.

Holding her was what broke me. The trust in her eyes that begged me not to drop her or hurt her was what terrified me. While most would be flattered that a baby is following them willingly, I swallow down my hysteria so that I can keep my job and dignity.

I would like to preface that I do not hate children. I love children, I enjoy taking care of them, seeing them smile and laugh, the feeling is rewarding. I am simply scared of myself. Perhaps my fear of children comes from the fact that I do not want them; hence, I do everything in my power to make sure that fate never happens. Maybe the fact that I do not want my children to inherit any of my worst traits, aka: stubbornness, perfectionism, catastrophizing, a peanut allergy, depression, and existential dread. My job as a mother lies in taking care of and loving my child unconditionally. But as I grow, I am still learning to take care of myself and rid my conditions of love. I cannot love another version of myself when there are things I want to constantly change about myself. I fear that because of my flaws, my children will grow to hate me too. So, abstaining from children is the best way for this problem to never reach me.

Overall, as I transition from adolescence to adulthood, the topic of kids will always be on my mind, and, begrudgingly, on the minds of others. As of now, I get incredulous looks from older women when I state that I do not want kids. Of course, now I do not want kids. I'm 17 and filled with intelligence, ambition, and looks you do not get to witness every day. Once I get pregnant, all of those things vanish, and I am left with my own regrets and a crying sack of flesh on my hip. All the while, these women will look at me and say, "You'll change your mind eventually," paired with blank eyes, a condescending smile, and a nagging sack of flesh stuck to their hand.
